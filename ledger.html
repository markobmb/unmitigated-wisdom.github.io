↳ #56 16 Jul 2021 · 00:03 view on Telegram →
People often associate government crackdowns with loss of value in crypto market. However, crackdowns decrease number of miners, lower hash difficulty, and thereby increase the reward for those who take risk. This is similar to other cases of law enforcement, eg. drugs are more expensive due to risk of arrest, incentivizing dealers to take that risk. but digital operations are much easier to cover up.
This logic also applies on a global scale. If 90% of countries enforce mining bans, they increase the reward for the other 10%, incentivizing them to continue their mining.
The only way this cycle could break is 1) if the miners are so few that the network becomes vulnerable to double spending. 2) the negative image (dirty mining) causes such a public backlash that will counter the limited supply.
