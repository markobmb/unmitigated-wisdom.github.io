What this site is

Why it exists

Most commentary on geopolitics and markets is unfalsifiable by design — vague enough to never be wrong, hedged enough to never be measured. Unmitigated Wisdom was started in 2020 on the opposite premise: every claim worth making is a claim someone could be wrong about, and the only honest way to build authority over time is to put forecasts in writing, date them, source them, and let events do the scoring.

Six years on, the project has grown from a single Telegram feed of falsifiable calls into a small analytical operation: original insight-driven forecasts on the channel, formal Bayesian scenario models on the report side, and a public scoreboard tracking how 64 outside analysts have done on 501 of their own predictions. Everything is kept in the open. Nothing is quietly deleted.

Methodology

What counts as a prediction

Predictions are only counted when they meet four standards:
  • Falsifiable — a concrete outcome that can be checked.
  • Forward-looking — made before the event, not after.
  • Specific — scoped enough to fail.
  • Attributable — tied to a named source with a citation.
