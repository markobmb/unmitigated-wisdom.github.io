Part III · Assessment

Interpreting the Decline

Separates curve-based inferences from external claims. Weight accordingly.

Explanation A

Deliberate Rationing

Curve-based evidence

D2–4 staircase (98, 89, 87). Drone surge-retreat (42→156→42). Saudi retargeting (0→47%). Maritime weapon-environment matching. Khorramshahr-4 innovation.

Explanation B

Degraded Capability

Curve-based evidence

Missiles fell ~80% vs drones ~60–70% from early-war levels. But differential also compatible with rational scarce-asset conservation.

External claims (not from counts)

IDF: 300+ launchers destroyed. CENTCOM: 80%+ Israel-range launchers eliminated. Firing shift to central Iran. Cooper: "lost ability to launch at high rates."

Official wartime damage assessments are historically prone to overstatement.
Part III · Assessment

Ship Attacks: Strait of Hormuz

21 confirmed attacks by Mar 12 (UKMTO). Too sparse for rate analysis but shows weapon-environment matching: missiles in Strait, USVs at range, fast boats in shallows. Post-Mar-11 cessation may reflect achieved closure, reduced traffic, or operational pause.

What the Curves Suggest

The strongest signal is the divergence between weapon categories. The most defensible inference is selective adaptation within unevenly constrained strike systems. Missiles more constrained; drones more flexible; maritime arm shows continued adaptation.
